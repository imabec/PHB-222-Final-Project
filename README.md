

## Research Question 

Does education have stronger health effects for women than men? 

Does education have stronger health effects for non-Hispanic whites than minority adults? 

## Variables 

To measure education, we will use (doesn't graduate high school, graduates high school, doesn't graduate college, graduates college).
To measure health effects, we will use (cardio, diabetes, mental).

Diabetes outcome variable: We have a variable that the respondant answers yes/no if they have diabetes 
Cardio outcome variable: We are going to create our own threshold based on the questionnaire answers (no cardio problems, moderate cardio problems, severe cardio problems)
Mental outcome variable: We are going to create our own threshold based on the questionnaire answers (no mental health problems, moderate mental health problems, severe mental health problems)

*Note: The variable "conditions" has a lot of good questions that we could use for outcome variables.*

## Confounders

We do want to control for income, race, gender, age. 

We don't want to control for physical activity, diet, drugs, cholestral, disability, food security, housing, kidney, occupation.
